How To Crochet

In recent years there has been a resurgence of interest in fiber arts such as crocheting, knitting, quilting, embroidery etc. A new generation of people are learning and exploring the creative and therapeutic potentials of these age old crafts. As someone who loves these generational trends and loves to crochet I want my how to guide to be an all inclusive beginners guide to crocheting, discussing not only the technical side of techniques, materials, and patterns, but also encouraging people to try new things, connect with communities, and learn about the benefits crafts like this can bring to someones life

Materials

Yarn

  • You can get good yarn in any craft store or online
  • Wool yarn is a good choice for beginners since it is easy to work with
  • For cheap yarn you can look at a local goodwill they often have bags of cheap yarn

Hooks

  • Generally the thicker your yarn the thicker the hook size
  • For beginners bigger hooks may be easier to work with
  • You can good crochet hooks from any craft store or online

Scissors

  • Any scissors suitable for cutting yarn will work

Stitch markers

  • This is a small marker you can place to keep track of specific stitches in your project
  • You can by from a store/online but can alternatively use any sort of makeshift marker

Yarn Needle

  • A large sized needle suitable for yarn you will use to weave in stray yarn

Stitches

Chain Stitch

make a slip stitch knot

Secure the knog to the hook

Yarn over and pull through. Repeat the steps to create a chain

Single Crochet

Insert hook into the last stitch

Yarn over the hook and pull through. You should have two loops on the hook afterwards.

Yarn over again and pull through the two loops on the hook. You should be left with one loop on the hook. Repeat

Double Crochet

Start with the steps from single crochet, but instead of pulling through the loops at the end leave all 3 loops on your crochet hook.

Yarn over and pull through the first two loops on your hook. You should be left with 2 loops.

Yarn over again and pull through the remaining two loops. You should be left with one loop. Repeat
